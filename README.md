## INTRODUCTION
The COVID-19 pandemic reshaped social, economic, and health systems across the world, making it one of the most significant global events of the past decade. Because the impact of the virus varied widely between regions and over time, understanding how cases and deaths evolved has been essential for governments, health organizations, and the public. For this project, we chose the Our World in Data COVID-19 dataset because it provides one of the most complete and widely used public records of pandemic statistics, covering more than 200 countries with daily updates on cases, deaths, testing, vaccination, and population metrics. However, for our project, we'll focus on the cases and deaths variables.

## DATA

## RESEARCH QUESTIONS
### Main research question.

- How does the relationship between COVID-19 cases and deaths vary across countries and over time?

Examining this relationship is important because cases alone do not fully describe the severity of the pandemic. Countries with similar case numbers often experienced very different mortality outcomes due to differences in health-care capacity, population size, vaccination rates, etc. By comparing these patterns at multiple geographic scales, we are able to highlight where outbreaks were most severe, when mortality risk increased, and how these patterns changed throughout the pandemic.

That is why our dashboard includes a set of visualizations, each designed to answer a specific question of interest, helping us interpret different aspects of the pandemic. We will outline each of these questions as we look at each graph.
